Current Route Ideas:
The main route travels through key locations in the southern Alaska region, focusing on the most scenic areas (reachable by road): Denali and the Kenai Peninsula. An optional excursion eastward towards Tock, Wrangell-St. Elias National Park, and the Eastern edge of the Alaska Mnt. range is up for debate. Assuming a stop at each town, the trip would take 8 days of travel (comfortable minimum) but likely we would want more to actually spend significant time at certain places and see unplanned attractions. (Total time can be better determined later into the planning stage.)
It should be mentioned that the majority of drives between destinations are only 1-2 hours. The largest stretch is four hourse, returning to Anchorage from Homer.
Additionally, Alaska allows you to legally pull over and camp/sleep if you need to
But if you'd prefer a more structured system, there are also plenty of campgrounds.
Campgrounds Map
Additionally, I think it would be an exciting opportunity/adventure to take a flight up to Utqiagvik/Barrow Alaska, the most northern town in the US. Its above the artic circle and has (during the summer) 24 hours of solid sunlight. A large amount of remote destinations must be reached by air travel and there are Alaska specific airlines just for these destinations. (It case you want to lobby for Nome, or Kotezbue)
Its a quick flight too, only 2 hours. We likely wouldn't spend too much time in Barrow (about a day and a half).
